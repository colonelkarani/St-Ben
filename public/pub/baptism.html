The First Sacrament

Baptism is the foundation of Christian life, the gateway to life in the Spirit, and the door which gives access to the other sacraments. Through Baptism we are freed from sin and reborn as sons and daughters of God.

It is called Baptism after the central rite by which it is carried out: to baptize means to "immerse" in water. The baptized person is immersed into the death of Christ and rises with him as a "new creature."

Symbols of Baptism

Water

The waters of Baptism symbolically represent both death and life. Going down into the water represents Christ's death and burial, rising from the water represents his resurrection to new life.

Holy Spirit

The Holy Spirit descends upon the newly baptized, just as the Spirit descended upon Jesus at his baptism in the Jordan River, anointing them with divine life.

Candle

The baptismal candle, lit from the Paschal candle, represents the light of Christ that now illuminates the newly baptized and the call to keep this divine light burning brightly.

White Garment

The white garment symbolizes that the baptized has "put on Christ" and has risen with Christ. It represents the Christian dignity and the purity of soul that must be preserved throughout life.

The Baptismal Journey

Preparing for Baptism

For infants, parents and godparents participate in preparation sessions to understand the commitment being made. For adults, the Rite of Christian Initiation for Adults (RCIA) provides formation in the faith.

The Celebration

The rite of Baptism includes the welcoming of the candidate, Scripture readings, prayers of exorcism and blessing, anointing with oil, the baptism with water, anointing with chrism, clothing with a white garment, and lighting of the baptismal candle.

Living the Baptismal Call

Baptism initiates us into a lifelong journey of faith. The baptized are called to grow in their relationship with Christ and to live out their commitment to the Gospel values in their daily lives.

Common Questions

Who can be baptized?

The Catholic Church baptizes both infants and adults. Anyone not yet baptized can receive the sacrament of Baptism after proper preparation.

What is the role of godparents?

Godparents are spiritual guides who help the baptized person live out their baptismal promises. They must be practicing Catholics who have received the sacraments of Baptism, Eucharist, and Confirmation.

When are baptisms celebrated?

Baptisms are typically celebrated on Sundays, either during Mass or in a separate ceremony. Special arrangements can be made for other days by contacting the parish office.

What documents are needed?

Parents need to provide a birth certificate for the child. Godparents may need to provide proof of their Catholic sacraments. Adults seeking baptism will discuss requirements during RCIA.
